— L'Anatomie de la Pierre

Five regions decide
whether a stone sings.

A round brilliant is fifty-eight facets in conversation. Where they meet — and at what angle — is the difference between glitter and fire.

I · Table
The plateau of light

The flat upper facet through which the eye enters the stone. Too wide and the diamond loses fire; too narrow and it loses spread.

II · Crown
Where fire begins

Thirty-three facets between table and girdle. The angles are minutes apart from one another — and from greatness.

III · Girdle
The equator

The narrow band where crown meets pavilion. Cut too thin and the stone is fragile; too thick and weight hides where light cannot reach.

IV · Pavilion
Where light is returned

The lower cone. The angle of 40.75° is the secret hinge of the round brilliant — a half-degree astray and brilliance collapses.

V · Culet
The vanishing point

The smallest facet — sometimes a single point. A great house leaves it closed: no leakage of light through the floor of the stone.
